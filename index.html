Terms

Payment Terms Overview

Structured instruments, clear inspection language, and controlled execution.

Accepted instruments

  • Irrevocable DLC/RDLC paid 100% at sight (SGS at loading port)
  • SBLC of 110% shipment value (security; typical validity 13 months)
  • 95/5 TT or DLC structures where applicable (subject to deal structure)

Instruments must be issued/confirmed by recognized banks acceptable to the seller.

Performance bond & inspection

  • Performance Bond issued after confirmed LC (subject to seller terms)
  • Independent surveyor (e.g., SGS) at loading port (seller cost)
  • Buyer representative welcome; optional checks at buyer cost

Pricing is indicative and subject to movement. Price is fixed on valid contract execution.

FAQ

Questions & Answers

Short answers to common trade questions (so we can focus on execution).

Will you accept non-transferable LC or EFT?

It depends on the seller’s funding and structure. Some deals require specific instruments due to mine/funder arrangements.

Why do some deals require transferable instruments?

Trade structures may include multiple beneficiaries (seller, mine, funder, freight agents). Transferable structures can coordinate payment against strict documents.

What quantities can you offer?

Quantities depend on grade and availability. Often a trial quantity is possible, then volumes increase based on buyer requirements.

Will you accept 100% after CIQ?

Typically no — many structures require payment at sight at the loading port, with quality control at loading to reduce disputes.

Can a buyer obtain past documents?

Sanitized documents may be shared after qualification and usually after contract signature, depending on seller discretion.

Is a mine visit permitted?

Usually yes, depending on the mine and deal stage. Costs can be borne by the buyer for accompaniment.
